# 🗳️ Decoding Tamil Nadu Assembly Election 2026  
## 📺 Domain: Media / Broadcasting  

---

# 🎯 Problem Statement  

Atliq Media, a national news network, wants to create a simple and fact-based election analysis show using publicly available election data.  

This project focuses on understanding:  

- How voter turnout changed between 2021 and 2026  
- Which regions changed politically  
- Which constituencies became more competitive  
- How a newly emerged political party changed the election results  

The goal is to convert raw election data into simple and meaningful insights using data analysis and storytelling.

The analysis covers:

- Close Contests → **102**  
- Landslide Wins → **517**  

---

# 🗳️ Q1: Did voter participation increase between 2021 and 2026?  

- 📊 2021 Turnout: **77.02%**  
- 📊 2026 Turnout: **85.83%**  
- 📈 Change: **+8.81%**  

### 🌍 Regional turnout changes:  
- Chennai Metro: **+17.11% (highest increase)**  
- Kongu Region: **+11.22%**  

👉 Urban and industrial regions showed a stronger rise in voter participation, which directly influenced competitiveness in many constituencies.  

---

# 🏛️ Q2: Who controlled Tamil Nadu in 2021 vs 2026?  

### 📌 2021 Election  
- INDIA Alliance: **159 seats**  
- Result: Clear majority government  

### 📌 2026 Election  
- TVK: **108 seats (largest party)**  
- INDIA Alliance: **73 seats**  
- NDA Alliance: **53 seats**  
- Majority mark: **118 seats (not reached by any single party)**  

👉 Tamil Nadu moved from a single-party majority system → to a fragmented, coalition-driven mandate.  

---

# 🌍 Q3: How did regional political dominance change?  

### 📌 2021 Dominance  
DMK-led INDIA alliance dominated:  
- South  
- North  
- Central  
- Chennai Metro  
- Delta  

### 📌 2026 Shift  
TVK emerged dominant in:  
- South  
- North  
- Chennai Metro  
- Kongu  

👉 Regional strongholds weakened, and political influence became more distributed across parties.  

---

# 🔁 Q4: Which regions saw the biggest political shift?  

### 🔴 South Tamil Nadu  
- Seat flips: **58 → 38**  
- Flip rate: **65.52%**  

### 🔵 Chennai Metro  
- Seat flips: **32 → 30**  
- Flip rate: **93.75%**  

👉 Chennai Metro experienced the highest volatility, while South remained the most competitive battleground.  

---

# ⚖️ Q5: Where were elections most competitive?  

### 🔥 Closely contested seats:  
- South: **30 seats**  
- Central: **20 seats**  
- North: **20 seats**  
- Chennai Metro: **2 seats**  

### 📊 Moderate contests:  
- South: **18 seats**  
- Central: **14 seats**  
- Delta: **14 seats**  

👉 South acted as the core battleground region, while Chennai Metro leaned toward more decisive outcomes.  

---

# 🏁 Q6: Where were landslide wins seen?  

- Chennai Metro: **10 landslide seats**  
- Chennai Metro also had **17 comfortable wins**  

👉 Urban constituencies showed clearer dominance patterns compared to other regions.  

---

# 🔄 Q7: How did political alliances shift across regions?  

- Chennai Metro: INDIA lost ~30 seats impact, TVK gained strongly  
- North Tamil Nadu: INDIA lost ~17 seat influence, TVK gained +15 seats  
- Kongu Region: NDA lost 13 seats, TVK gained 16 seats  
- South Tamil Nadu: TVK gained 26 seats, both INDIA & NDA declined  
- Central & Delta: TVK gained 12 and 10 seats respectively  

👉 TVK’s growth was statewide, not regional — a uniform expansion across Tamil Nadu.  

---

# 📊 Q8: Which constituencies changed the most?  

### 🔥 High-impact seat changes:  
- Salem (West): **22.10% margin shift (PMK → TVK)**  
- Viralimalai: **18.30% margin shift (stable seat, strong swing)**  
- Sivaganga: **1.50% margin shift (AIADMK → TVK)**  
- Palladam: **0.00% margin shift (razor-thin flip)**  
- Saidapet: **-0.50% margin shift (DMK → TVK)**  

👉 Even extremely small vote shifts decided major outcomes.  

---

# 📈 Q9: How did turnout impact results?  

### 📊 High turnout constituencies:  
- Villivakkam: **+28.7% turnout | -13.8% margin shift**  
- Kolathur: **+22.4% turnout | -36.2% margin shift**  

👉 Higher turnout often tightened margins and increased competition  

### 📊 Turnout strengthening wins:  
- Velachery: **+26.3% turnout | +15.7% margin shift**  
- Sholinganallur: **+20.1% turnout | +12.5% margin shift**  

👉 In some cases, higher turnout strengthened the winning side instead of weakening it  

---

# 📉 Q10: Were there stable constituencies?  

- Kiliyoor: **2.2% turnout | -32% margin shift**  
- Ramanathapuram: **3.2% turnout | -18.4% margin shift**  
- Edappadi: **3.2% turnout | +0.1% margin shift**  

👉 These reflect strong voter loyalty and stable political behavior  

---

# 🧠 Final Insight  

If we step back from the numbers, a clear pattern emerges:  

- Turnout increased significantly (**+8.81%**)  
- 163 constituencies changed hands  
- A new political force (TVK) rose to **108 seats**  
- Traditional strongholds weakened across regions  
- Urban areas became more volatile than rural regions  
- The state moved into a multi-party competitive system  

👉 Final conclusion:  

> This election was not just about winning seats — it was about how voter behavior reshaped the entire political map of Tamil Nadu.
